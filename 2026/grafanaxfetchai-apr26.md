## From APIs to A2A: Observability for AI Agents by [Kumbhaj Shukla](https://linkedin.com/in/kumbhaj)
  - Backend, DB, Infrastructure > Runs behind the scene of a Production-based system
  - Infrastructure is empowered by DevOps and Observability
  - Currently, most of our production systems are interacting with each other using APIs
  - Surface level logging and Status based logging were mostly enough for observability of APIs
  - A2A is Agent-to-Agent protocol was introduced by Google in 2025 to enable interaction between different agents dealing with specific use-cases.
  - Future Stack would mostly be MCP and A2A.
  - OpenTelemetry is actively working on getting observability aligned for A2A and MCPs.
  - [Langfuse](https://langfuse.com/) is a MIT, self-hostable, track trees + scoring framework. [MLFlow Tracing](https://mlflow.org/docs/latest/genai/tracing/) is a similar open-source platform.
  - A2A-Native Observability using Rich-Telementry can be utilized at a scale.

## Monitoring Intelligent Systems with Grafana by [Sarika Sharma](https://linkedin.com/in/sarikasharma1717)
  - Logs, Metrics & Traces are the key pillars of observability.
  - Grafana unifies all these three into a single pane of glass making it easy to observe and debug systems/agents.
  - AI Agent Modules performs actions and then sends the logs and detailed metrics of the events. The Grafana module helps in visualizing and monitoring it. Finally, Insights module helps us to get the summarize it.
